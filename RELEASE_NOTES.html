Chakra - 2010-09-20 - Version: 0.2.1.0920-kde445


The Chakra Developement Team is proud to announce the availability of our next
point-release of Jaz, the live system using our stable repositories.

"All that jazz"

We have been working hard since March 2010 on Chakra GNU/Linux.

10 Developers hacking, packaging, writing documentation, scripts, and having fun furthering our new distribution. Chakra is based on ArchLinux, but it is not ArchLinux. In fact, it's a new type of GNU/Linux. Offering several common styles of software management, and a single supported interface (KDE SC), along with safe, stable, and well tested upgrades. We are using pacman for now, but working on our own advanced qt/sql package managment library called Akabei. Since the split we created some confusion but we are going in the right direction.

Over 5670 packages are now available for our new distribution and over 500 get installed by this installer.

To showcase our new software bundling system, the mechanism for click-n-run and seperate-from-system apps, we created Cinstall - a simple multi-tool to create and manage our bundles & packages. It can browse the repositories, install/remove packages, launch bundles, create bundles, etc. All these features you'll find later in Shaman.

We stabilized Tribe (this installer) and added some changes to get it even better than the last release, but of course, it's still alpha software.

More hands on deck are always welcome.


What is under the hood?

ArchLinux users will feel right at home from the start. The core system is the same from ArchLinux. We have our own patched kernel to fit the needs of a desktop/pc system.

All packages are compiled and reviewed by our team to get a rock-solid stable system.

We still use pacman (and Cinstall) for software management for the time being.


What programs come with the install?

We want to give you a minimal full functional KDE Desktop you can build on. We have all you can imagine of programs and plasmoids from the KDE-World in our repositories. Simply use cinstall or pacman to search them.


Here few to point out:

    * BlueDevil - manage your bluetooth devices
    * K3B - burn your data
    * Bangarang - watch your medias or TV
    * Networkmanager-Plasmoid - connect to the internet

Build-Notes

For the time being, use nvidia-xconfig to configure your nvidia graphics, instead of nvidia-settings, due to the gtk dependency, as this image is gtk free.

You can install packages from our repositories with pacman, or cinstall.

Read more about gtk-freeness here.


System Requirements:

    * an Intel Pentium II or higher processor or AMD64-ready CPU for 64bit
